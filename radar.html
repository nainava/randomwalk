Random Walk: Radar

The atlas catalogs breakthroughs after the fact. The radar catalogs active work at crossings between distant fields before outputs arrive. Sources scanned include lab project portfolios, researcher training histories, grant programs, and paper citations.

Each entry is a person or lab positioned at a crossing between two fields. Entries are tagged by stage: configuration (structural conditions exist, no output), in progress (active work), early output (paper or result published). Method validated retroactively against 11 Atlas breakthroughs: 10 of 11 flagged, ~3.5 year average lead time.

Ara Varma is a designer based in San Francisco. She was an Associate at SOSV ($1.5b deep tech VC) and worked in sales at Loft Orbital. In these roles, she encountered many people on their own random walks, building interesting science.

This project catalogues and formalizes what many know to be true: funding research in one area often leads to value in unexpected areas. We document the positive unintended consequences of high-intensity research, and seek to understand how we can support more.
