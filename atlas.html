Mechanisms
accident
An unplanned signal drifts in from outside the experiment; the researcher notices. Penicillin: mold contaminated a plate.
salvaged failure
The planned experiment ran as designed but produced the wrong result; the researcher kept the wrong result. Super Glue: formula failed as a gunsight, stuck to everything.
side effect
A treatment developed for condition A turns out to do something useful for unrelated condition B. Viagra: angina trial, patients reported erections.
cross pollination
A researcher who sits at the intersection of two fields imports method knowledge from one into the other. GLP-1: an endocrinologist uses reptile venom biology to probe human gut hormones.
tool migration
A concrete artifact — instrument, dataset, codebase, infrastructure — built for task A gets reused for task B. Transformer: translation architecture becomes the backbone of general-purpose AI.
analogy
A pattern observed in nature or another domain is deliberately borrowed as a design. Velcro: burrs on a dog's fur become a fastener.

Ara Varma is a designer based in San Francisco. She was an Associate at SOSV ($1.5b deep tech VC) and worked in sales at Loft Orbital. In these roles, she encountered many people on their own random walks, building interesting science.

This project catalogues and formalizes what many know to be true: funding research in one area often leads to value in unexpected areas. We document the positive unintended consequences of high-intensity research, and seek to understand how we can support more.
