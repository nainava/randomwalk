Cabinet of Curiosities

Can we brute force random scientific discovery?

Can we “predict” random scientific discovery?

We are not sure. This website will be updated to highlight the people trying. In the meantime, we’ve collected a list of curiosities that may be worth learning more about.

These are weird things happening in science right now that nobody knows what to do with. Unexpected results that don’t fit models. Properties without applications. Failed experiments with interesting residuals. Observations sitting in one field that would be startling to people in another.

The history of breakthroughs is the history of people who noticed something weird and decided to poke at it instead of moving on.
