Cabinet of Curiosities

107 observations from the edges of settled science — anomalies, unexplained properties, and cross-field collisions that may be worth a closer look.

These are not predictions. They are curiosities — findings that sit in the gap between what has been observed and what has been explained. Each one was noticed by someone, published somewhere, and then largely left alone. Some may turn out to be noise. Others may be the beginning of something.
